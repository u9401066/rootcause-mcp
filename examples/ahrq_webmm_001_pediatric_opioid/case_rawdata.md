# AHRQ WebM&M Case #001: Pediatric Post-Surgical Pain Management

> **Source**: AHRQ Patient Safety Network - WebM&M
> **Case Type**: Medication Error / Adverse Drug Event
> **Setting**: Outpatient / Emergency Department
> **Date**: 2025

---

## Case Description

### Patient Information

- **Age**: 2-year-old male
- **Medical History**: Previously healthy
- **Procedure**: Uncomplicated urologic surgery for hypospadias repair

---

### Timeline of Events

#### Day 0 - Post-Surgery Discharge

The patient underwent uncomplicated urologic surgery for hypospadias repair. For pain control, the patient was prescribed:

- **Medication**: Hydrocodone-Acetaminophen 7.5-325 mg/15 mL solution
- **Dose**: 2.2 mL per dose (1.1 mg hydrocodone + 48 mg acetaminophen)
- **Frequency**: By mouth, 4 times daily as needed for pain
- **Duration**: 5-day course
- **Follow-up**: Scheduled for 13 days after surgery

---

#### Day 2 - Emergency Department Visit

Two days after surgery, the patient was brought to the Emergency Department due to **inconsolable crying**.

**ED Workup**:
- Examination was unremarkable
- Patient deemed appropriate for discharge

**Prescription Change**:
- **New Medication**: Oxycodone 1 mg/mL solution
- **Dose**: 2.3 mL per dose (0.2 mg/kg/dose)
- **Frequency**: By mouth, every 4 to 6 hours
- **Total Quantity Dispensed**: 64.4 mL (4.6-day supply)

---

#### Day 6 - Adverse Event (4 days after ED discharge)

The patient became:
- Apneic (stopped breathing)
- Cyanotic (turned blue)
- Unresponsive

**Emergency Response**:
- Emergency first responders called to scene
- Cardiac rhythm: Pulseless Electrical Activity (PEA)
- Interventions provided:
  - Cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR)
  - Naloxone administered
  - Two doses of epinephrine

**Outcome**:
- Upon arrival at ED, continued resuscitation was unsuccessful
- Child was declared deceased
- **High suspicion**: Cardiopulmonary arrest due to opioid overdose

---

## Documentation Gaps Noted

1. Family education on opioid use or adverse effects was **not documented** at initial surgical discharge
2. Family education was **not documented** upon ED discharge
3. Naloxone was **not prescribed** on either occasion
